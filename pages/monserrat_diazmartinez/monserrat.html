My Internship Experience

This summer I intered at the Vaan group, they design and redesign webpages for various different companies, mainly e-commerce.

My internship experience was fun. There was only two other workers in the office for most of the time since everyone else was working remotely, so I didn't get the typical big office experience like others but I still enjoyed it for my first work experience.

I got to see a variety of aspects of a business; I attended client meetings and saw how to give presentations on designs and how important it is to give options, I saw what UX research is like for redesigning an e-commerce website and what kinds of feedback is used to better the site, I got a glimpse of what creating a new product brand is like and got to hear about different experiences-and recieve advice-that my coworkers had.

What I Worked On

I worked on various different things...

  • Taking the Shopify (an e-commerce platform) fundamentals courses in order to learn essential business knowledge as well as how to create an effective e-commerce site
    • I learned things such as...
      • project management methodologies
      • pricing strategies
      • sales strategies and how to manage risks
      • how to market your self and your client
      • approaches for theme development of a Shopify site
      • an overview of Shopify's APIs used to customize the virtual storefront and functionality
  • Doing market research to create a competitive landscape—a collection of information about realted brands, their products, and what makes them unique
  • Creating a mood board that contributed to strengthening brand image
  • Participated in interviews conducted to gauge how redesigns were going from a user's perspective
  • Attending meetings where designs were presented to clients and gave feedback
  • Learning Stripe, a way to collect payment information, to implement a donation button
  • Contributing to a Wordpress website and discovering different plug-ins to achieve necessary look and function

Key Takeaways

There's a lot that I am going to take away from this internship. I learned how many details are taken into account when working on a project. You need to have very clear communication with clients in order to give them what they want, and even then it could take several revisions before they are content. On that note, it's also important to give your opinion and stand your ground. You shouldn't have to lose your sanity just to please the client.

Another thing I learned was how many means there are to the same end. If you've already done something, or if someone else has done something, there's no need to remake the wheel; just pull out that old piece of work or download that plug-in and tailor it to what you need instead of starting from scratch each time.

One last thing I learned is how easy it is to just reach out to someone on the team for help. While it may have seemed intimidating to me at first, I quickly saw that even if you've never met, coworkers are available that have much more experience and therefore knowledge than you and I should take advantage of that. I also saw how valuable anyone can be to a project, when I went to meetings I was able to contribute and my opinions were actually respected.
